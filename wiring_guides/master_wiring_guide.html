Complete Step-by-Step Hardware Instructions for the Fruit Plucking Robot

⚠️ The 3 Unbreakable Rules of Robotics

  • The Ground Rule: Every single component (Pi, Arduino, Sensors, Motor Driver) MUST have their Ground (GND) wires connected together. If they do not share a ground, signals will fail and the robot will twitch.
  • The Voltage Rule: The Raspberry Pi GPIO pins can ONLY handle 3.3 Volts. If you feed 5 Volts into a GPIO pin (like from an Ultrasonic sensor), the Pi will instantly die. Use resistors!
  • The Motor Power Rule: NEVER power motors or servos from the Raspberry Pi or Arduino pins. They draw too much current. Use your 12V Battery and 5V Buck Converters.

1 The Power Foundation

Before plugging in data wires, we must establish safe power lines from the battery.

ComponentPositive (+)Negative (-)Notes
1. SmartElex Motor Driver 12V Battery directly Battery GND Motors need pure 12V.
2. Buck Converter (INPUT) 12V Battery directly Battery GND TUNE TO 5.0V WITH SCREWDRIVER BEFORE NEXT STEP!
3. Raspberry Pi 4 Buck 5V OUT → Pi Pin 2 Buck GND OUT → Pi Pin 6 The Pi is now safely powered.
4. PCA9685 Servo Driver Buck 5V OUT → V+ Terminal Buck GND OUT → GND Terminal This powers the arm's heavy servos safely.
5. Arduino Uno USB Cable from Raspberry Pi USB Cable Pi powers and talks to Arduino via USB!
