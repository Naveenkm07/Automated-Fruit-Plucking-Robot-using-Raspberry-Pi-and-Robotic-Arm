🦾 Step 5: The Robotic Arm

Connecting the 6 Servo Motors using the PCA9685 Servo Driver

How it works:

Your robot uses 6 different servo motors for the arm. Instead of plugging 6 messy wires into the Arduino, your code uses the PCA9685 16-Channel Servo Driver Board (the small blue board with lots of pins).

The Arduino only needs 2 wires to talk to this board, and the board handles all 6 servos perfectly!

1 Power the Servo Board (V+ and GND)

⚠️ SERVO POWER IS CRITICAL! Do NOT power the servos from the Arduino's 5V pin, or the Arduino will burn out!

You must use the 5V output from your Buck Converter and connect it to the large Green Terminal block on the PCA9685 board.

From: 5V Buck ConverterTo: PCA9685 Green Terminal Block
Positive 5V Wire (+)Connect to V+
Negative Wire (-)Connect to GND

2 Connect the Arduino to the Servo Board

Now we connect the "Brain" to the board using I2C communication (just 4 wires).

PCA9685 Side PinsArduino Uno Pins
VCC (Logic Power)5V
GND (Logic Ground)GND
SDA (Data)A4 (or SDA pin)
SCL (Clock)A5 (or SCL pin)

3 Plug in the 6 Servos

This is the easy part! Simply plug the 3-wire connectors from your servos into the numbered slots on the PCA9685 board. Make sure the Brown wire faces the bottom (GND) and the Yellow/Orange wire faces the top (PWM).

PCA9685 Slot NumberWhich Servo Goes Here?Servo Type

Channel 1Shoulder Lift (Lifts the whole arm)MG996R (Big)
