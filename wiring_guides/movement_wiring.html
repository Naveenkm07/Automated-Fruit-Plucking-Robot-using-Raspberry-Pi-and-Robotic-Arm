🚗 Step 3: Movement (SmartElex & Wheels)

Connecting the Raspberry Pi to the Motor Driver to control the wheels

How it works:

The Raspberry Pi cannot spin the motors itself. It uses tiny 3.3V signals to tell the SmartElex 15S Motor Driver what to do. The SmartElex takes those tiny signals, and acts like a massive switch to unleash the 12V battery power into the actual wheels!

1 Connect Motors to SmartElex (The Output)

Your rover has 4 wheels, but they act like a tank (Left side and Right side).

  • Wire the two LEFT motors together (parallel). Plug them into the MOTOR A (or Left Motor) terminal on the SmartElex.
  • Wire the two RIGHT motors together (parallel). Plug them into the MOTOR B (or Right Motor) terminal on the SmartElex.
💡 If you test the robot later and it spins in circles instead of going forward, you simply flip the two wires on the terminal block for whichever side is going backwards!

2 Connect Pi to SmartElex (The Input Signals)

Now we connect the signal wires. We use "PWM" to control the SPEED of the wheels, and "DIR" to control the DIRECTION (forward/backward) of the wheels.

SmartElex 15S PinFunctionConnect to Raspberry Pi
Left PWMControls Left SpeedGPIO 12 → Pin 32
Left DIRControls Left DirectionGPIO 5 → Pin 29
Right PWMControls Right SpeedGPIO 13 → Pin 33
Right DIRControls Right DirectionGPIO 6 → Pin 31

3 The Most Important Wire (Ground)

⚠️ Without this wire, your rover will twitch uncontrollably or not move at all!

For the Raspberry Pi to send signals to the SmartElex, they MUST share a common Ground connection so they have the same electrical reference point.

From: SmartElexTo: Raspberry Pi
GND (Ground)Any GND Pin → Pin 39
✅ Great job! Your robot now has legs and can move!
