🔋 Step 1: The Power System Wiring

Learn how to power the Brains, the Motors, and the Arm without frying anything.

The Golden Rule of Robotics Wiring:
Motors and Servos draw HUGE amounts of electricity. If you connect motors directly to your Raspberry Pi, the Pi will crash, restart, or burn out. We solve this by taking power from the 12V Battery, and splitting it into different paths using Buck Converters (which step 12V down to a safe 5V).

Wiring Flowchart

12V Battery
│ ├──────────────── 12V (+) & GND (-) ─────────────┐ │ │ ▼ ▼
SmartElex Motor Driver
(For the wheels - needs 12V)
Buck Converter
(Steps 12V down to 5V)
│ ├──────────────── 5V (+) & GND (-) ──────────┐ │ │ ▼ ▼
Raspberry Pi & Arduino
(The Brains - strictly 5V)
Robotic Arm Servos
(The Muscles - strictly 5V)
* ALL GROUND (GND) WIRES IN THE ENTIRE ROBOT MUST BE CONNECTED TOGETHER! *

1 Connect the Wheels (12V)

The SmartElex 15S motor driver controls your heavy rover wheels, so it needs the full 12 Volts straight from the battery.

From: 12V BatteryTo: SmartElex 15S Motor Driver
Positive Wire (+)Connect to VIN (+) or 12V terminal block
Negative Wire (-)Connect to GND (-) terminal block

2 Set up the Buck Converter (12V to 5V)

The Raspberry Pi and Arduino will die instantly if you feed them 12V. You must route power through the Buck Converter.

⚠️ CRITICAL STEP: Before you plug the Buck Converter into the Raspberry Pi, you MUST use a screwdriver to turn the tiny screw on the Buck Converter until its output reads exactly 5.0V or 5.1V on a Multimeter!
From: 12V BatteryTo: Buck Converter (INPUT Side)
Positive Wire (+)Connect to IN+
Negative Wire (-)Connect to IN-

3 Power the Brains (5V)

Now that the Buck Converter is outputting a safe 5 Volts, we can power the Raspberry Pi and the Arduino.

From: Buck Converter (OUTPUT Side)To: Raspberry Pi 4
Positive 5V Wire (+) (OUT+)Connect to Physical Pin 2 or Pin 4 (5V Power)
Negative Wire (-) (OUT-)Connect to Physical Pin 6 (Ground)

Note: You can power the Arduino Uno by just plugging a USB cable from the Raspberry Pi into the Arduino! The Pi will share its 5V power and talk to the Arduino at the same time.

4 Power the Robotic Arm (5V)

The MG996R servos on the robotic arm draw a massive amount of current when lifting heavy tomatoes. If you power the servos directly from the Arduino or Raspberry Pi pins, the Pi will shut down (brownout) every time the arm moves.

From: Buck Converter (OUTPUT Side)To: Servo Motors (Red & Brown wires)
Positive 5V Wire (+) (OUT+)Connect to the RED wire (VCC) of all Servos
Negative Wire (-) (OUT-)Connect to the BROWN wire (GND) of all Servos

The YELLOW/ORANGE wire on the servos is the Signal wire. That is the ONLY wire that goes to the Arduino!

✅ Once this is done, the Power Foundation of your robot is complete!
